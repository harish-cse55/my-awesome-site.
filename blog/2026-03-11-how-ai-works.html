How AI Actually "Thinks": The Logic of Tokens

Most people think AI "reads" sentences like humans do. But as engineers, we know computers only understand numbers. To bridge this gap, AI uses a concept called Tokenization.

What is a Token?

A token is the basic unit of text that an AI processes. It isn't always a full word; it can be a part of a word, a single character, or even punctuation.

How are Tokens Calculated?

The general rule of thumb for English is that 1,000 tokens ≈ 750 words. AI models like GPT-4 or Gemini use a "Vocabulary" of about 100,000+ unique tokens. When you type, the AI converts your text into a sequence of integers (IDs) from this vocabulary.

From Tokens to Predictions

Once the text is turned into numbers, the Transformer Architecture takes over. The AI calculates the probability of the *next* token based on the previous ones. It is essentially a massive, high-speed math equation.

The Math Formula

The probability $P$ of a sequence is calculated as:

$$P(w_1, w_2, ..., w_n) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} P(w_i | w_1, ..., w_{i-1})$$

This is why AI can feel "creative"—it's choosing the most likely next piece of data based on trillions of patterns.

Why Tokens Matter for Devs

If you are building an app with an API (like my AlertMate backend), you pay per token. Understanding how to prune your prompts and minimize token usage is key to keeping costs low and speed high.
